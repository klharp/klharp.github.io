Project Summary

The Efficient Windows Collaborative (EWC) was a Department of Energy-sponsored project until 2013 when Kerry Harp and colleague, John Carmody, started an non-profit organization to continue the program's outreach and education. In 2020, the non-profit organization was purchased by the National Fenestration Rating Council (NFRC).

A key asset of the EWC was the website which was designed and maintained by Kerry Harp. The site was intended to be a non-biased source of information for energy-efficient fenestration products. Along with the educational sections, the site contained an application called the Window Selection Tool (WST). The WST was constructed in PHP with a MySQL database. The database consisted of annual energy use data for over 660,000 whole-house energy simulations. The database also contained data for comfort, condensation, costs, and manufacturer member information.

Notable items:

  • The website received up to 20,000 visitors per month.
  • The comfort metric was a first-ever effort to quantify comfort. The methodology was developed by Kerry Harp and Jim Larsen from Cardinal Glass Industries.
  • The audience for the site was the general consumers and window industry manufacturers.
  • All simulations were done with a generic set of 23 windows, which manufacturers could compare to their actual products.
